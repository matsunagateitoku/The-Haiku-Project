Takahama Kyoshi
高浜虚子  ·  31 poems
Spring
草を摘む子の野を渡る巨人かな
kusa wo tsumu / ko no no wo wataru / kyojin kana
Over the children / picking herbs on the fields something / gigantic passes.
芽ぐむなる大樹の幹に耳を寄せ
megumu naru / taiju no miki ni / mimi wo yose
It begins to bud— / close to the trunk of the great tree / I strain my eyes to listen.
ぱっと火になりたる蜘蛛や草を焼く
patto hi ni / nari-taru kumo ya / kusa wo yaku
In an instant / it has become a flame: a spider / in the burning grass.
春めきし野山消え去る夕かげり
haru mekishi / no yama kie saru / yu kageri
the vernal / fields and mountains fade away / into dusk shadows
啓蟄の蟻が早引く地虫かな
keichitsu no / ari ga hayahiku / jimushi kana
insects awake / ants already dragging / a grub
ただ霞む春の山ある遠眼鏡
tada kasumu / haru no yama aru / Tomegane
only misted / spring mountains are there / telescope
Summer
夕立を真横に走る白帆かな
yūdachi wo / mayoko ni hashiru / shiraho kana
The summer shower— / cutting straight through it, there goes / a white sailboat.
金亀子＿＿つ闇の深さかな
koganemushi / nagetsu yami no / fukasa kana
A gold bug— / I hurl it into the darkness / and feel the depth of the night.
蛇逃げて我を見し眼の草に残る
hebi nigete / ware wo mishi me no / fukasa kana
After the snake flees / the eyes that glared at me / remain in the grass.
夏の月皿の林檎の紅失す
natsu no tsuki / sara no ringo / kō shissu
The summer moon— / on the plate lies an apple / with its redness lost.
川を見るバナナ皮は手より落る
kawa wo miru / banana no kawa wa / te yori ochi
I gaze at the river. / A banana peel I held / slips from my hand.
白牡丹といふといへども紅ほのか
shira-botan / iu to ie domo / beni honoka
Even though / it is called a white peony, / a hint of crimson
二つ吊し　簾の透間　花柘榴
Futatsu tsuri shi / sudare no sukima / hana zakuro
Two blinds hung up, / and from between them, / pomegranate flowers.
風吹けば　来るや隣の　鯉幟
Kaze fukeba / kuru ya tonari no / koi nobori
When the wind blows, / they come! the neighbor's / carp banners.
彼一語　我一語　新茶　淹れながら
Kare ichigo / ware ichigo / shincha / ire nagara
He — a word, / I — a word, while making / new tea.
行水の　女にほれる　烏かな
Gyozui no / onna ni horeru / karasu kana
The tub-bathing / lady, he's fallen for — / Mr. Crow.
Autumn
裏戸より借るべき家の菊を見つ
urado yori / karu-beki ie no / kiku wo mitsu
Through the back gate / of the house I am to rent / chrysanthemums are seen.
桐一葉日当りながら落ちにえり
kiri hitoha / hi atari nagara / ochinikeri
A paulownia leaf / basking in the sunlight— / it’s dropped to the ground!
秋天の下に野菊の花弁欠く
shūten no / shita ni no-giku no / kaben kaku
Under the autumnal / sky, a wild chrysanthemum / lacking a petal.
天日のうつりて暗し＿＿の水
tenjitsu no / utsurite kurashi / kato no mizu
The sun in the sky / is mirrored darkly on the water / where tadpoles live.
もの置けばそこに生まれぬ秋の蔭
mono okeba / soko ni umarenu / aki no kage
When I set / something down, there emerges / autumn’s shadow.
彼一語我一語秋深みくも
kare ichigo / ware ichigo aki / fukami kamo
He says a word, / and I say a word—autumn / is deepening.
Winter
流れ行く大根の葉の早さかな
nagare yuku / daikon no ha no / hayasa kana
Floating away— / the turnip leaves—and how / swiftly the go!
凍蝶の己が魂追うて飛ぶ
itechō no / onoga tamashii / ōte tobu
A butterfly / in the cold: it flies in pursuit / of its own soul.
大寒や見舞に行けば死んでをり
daikan ya / mimai ni yukeba / shinde-ori
Midwinter’s cold: / I go to visit a sick man / —he is already dead!
枯菊に尚ほ或物をとどめずや
kare-giku ni / nao arumono wo / todomezu ya
A dead chrysanthemum / and yet—isn't there still something / remaining in it?
冬日今瞼のありて重たけれ
fuyubi ima / mabuta no arite / omota keri
Winter sunlight / now lying on my eyelids / seems heavy
Unclassified
harukaze ya / tōshi idakite / oka ni tatsu
In the spring wind, / Nursing my fighting spirit, / I stand on a hill.
kamakura wo / odorokashitaru / yokan ari
Cold of winter, / Still lingering, has astonished / All Kamakura.
winter
遠山に日のあたりたる枯野かな
tōyama ni / hi no atari-taru / kareno kana
On the distant hills / the rays of the sun fall… / a withered moor.
New Years
去年今年貫く棒の如きもの
kozo kotoshi / tsuranuku bō no / gotoki mono
Last year and this year— / piercing through the two, something / like a pole.
