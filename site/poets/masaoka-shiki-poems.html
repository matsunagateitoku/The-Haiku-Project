Spring
砂浜にきららの光る春日なか
sunahama ni / kirata no hikaru / haru hinaka
On a sandy beach / glassy chips sparkle / in the spring sunshine.
花散って水は南へ流れけり
hana chitte / mizu wa minami e / nagare-keri
Blossoms have fallen / and the water is flowing / towards the south.
雪解や竹はね返る日の表
yukidoke ya / take hanekaeru / hi no omote
The snow melts: / bamboos uncoil themselves / where the sunlight falls.
Summer
山の湯泉や裸の上の天の川
yama no yu ya / hadaka no ue no / ama-no-gawa
Hot spring in the mountains: / high above the naked bathers / the River of Heaven.
蛇逃げて山静かなり百合の花
hebi nigete / yama shizuka-nari / yuri no hana
After the snake flees, / how quiet the forest is! / A lily flower.
絶えず人いこふ夏野の石一つ
taezu hito / ikou natsuno no / ishi hitotsu
Unceasingly / this stone on the summer moor / rests people.
看護婦やうたた寝さめて蠅を打つ
kangofu ya / utatane samete / hae wo utsu
My nurse, / awakening from a nap, / swats a fly.
雲の峰硯に蟻の上りけり
kumo no mine / suzuri ni ari no / nobori-keri
A column of cloud— / onto my inkstone, an ant / has climbed.
愛憎は蠅打って蟻に与へけり
aizō wa / hae utte ari ni / atae-keri
For love and for hate / I swat a fly and offer it / to an ant.
Autumn
糸瓜咲いて痰のつまりし仏かな
hechima saite / tan no tsumarishi / hotoke kana
The sponge-gourd has flowered! / Look at the Buddha / Choked with phlegm.
鶏頭の十四五本もありぬべし
keitō no / jūshigo hon mo / arinu beshi
Cockscomb— / I'm sure there are at least / Fourteen or fifteen stalks.
低く飛ぶ畦のいなごや日の弱り
hikuku tobu / aze no inago ya / hi no yowari
See how low it flies. / That locust on the paddy walk— / The sunshine weakens.
hatsuaki no / ishidan takashi / sugi kodachi
The stone flight of steps / Looms high in early autumn / Amidst the cedars.
妹に群書よまする夜長かな
imotō ni / gunsho yomasuru / yonaga kana
My young sister / reads me an ancient war epic / this endless night.
秋寒し眼の光る鬼女の面
aki samushi / manako no hikaru / kijo no men
Autumn chill: / with eyes glaring, there hangs / the mask of a demoness.
蜘殺すあとのさびしき夜寒かな
kumo korosu / ato no sabishiki / yosamu kana
After killing / a spider how lonely I feel / in the cold of night!
馬下りて川の名問へば秋の風
uma orite / kawa no na toeba / aki no kaze
I get down from my horse / and ask the name of the river— / an autumnal wind.
世をいとふ心薊を愛すかな
yo wo itou / kokoro azami wo / aisu kana
The heart that loathes / this world thinks lovingly / of a thistle.
ある僧の月を待たずに帰りけり
aru sō no / tsuki wo matazu ni / kaeri-keri
A Buddhist monk / without waiting for the moonrise / takes his leave.
棚の糸瓜思ふ所へぶら下がる
tana no hechima / omou tokoro e / burasagaru
From the trellis / sponge gourds dangle down, each one / where it pleases.
ある僧の月を待たずの帰りけり
aru sō no / tsuki wo matazu no / kaeri keri
A certain monk / has gone without waiting / for the moon to rise
柿食えば鐘が鳴るなり法隆寺
kaki kueba / kane ga naru nari / hōryūji
I bite the persimmon / and the bell starts booming / Hōryūji Temple
Winter
木の影や我が影動く冬の月
ki no kage ya / waga kage ugoku / fuyu no tsuki
Shadows of the trees: / my shadow wavers with them / in the winter moonlight.
凩や鐘引きすてて道の端
The wintry gust: / they have left a temple bell / by the roadside.
The wintry gust: / they have left a temple bell / by the roadside.
いくたびも雪の深さを尋ねけり
ikutabi mo / yuki no fukusa wo / tazune keri
How many times / I asked how deep / the snow was
New Year
元日や枯菊残る庭のさき
ganjitsu ya / karegiku nokoru / niwa nosaki
New Year’s Day: / dead chrysanthemums remain / at the garden’s edge.
初暦五月の中に死ぬ日あり
hatsugoyomi / gogatsu no naka ni / shinu hi ari
New year’s calendar: / in the month of May there is / a day for dying.
Unclassified
akikane ni / koborete akashi / hamigakiko"
Spilling over / In the spring breeze, how red— / My tooth powder!
ikutabi mo / yuki no fukasa wo / tazunekeri
Again and again / I asked the others how much / Snow had fallen.
nagaki hi ya / roba wo oiyuku / muchi no kage
The long days— / the shadow of a lash / Chases a donkey.
