Spring
春立つや新年ふるき小目五升
haru tasu / shinnen furuki / go-shō
spring begins― / for the new year, five pints / of old rice
春雨や蜂の巣つたふ屋根の漏
harusame ya / hachi no su tsutau / yane no mori
spring rain― / along a bee nest / the roof leak
よく見ればなづな花咲く垣根哉
yoku mireba / nazuna hanasaku / kakine kana
look closely: / the shepherd's purse is / flowering at the hedge
いづる鶯　声波ヲうって　腸氷ル夜やなみだ
izuru uguisu / koe nami wo utte / harawata koōru yoru ya namida
the warbler emerges — / its cry strikes the waves, / a night of frozen entrails and tears
山路来て　何やらゆかし　すみれ草
yamaji kite / naniyara yukashi / sumire gusa
coming along a mountain path — / something strangely moving: / violets
古池や　蛙飛びこむ　水の音
furuike ya / kawazu tobikomu / mizu no oto
the ancient pond — / a frog leaps in, / sound of water
行く春や　鳥啼き　魚の目は泪
yuku haru ya / tori naki / sakana no me wa namida
spring going — / birds cry, / and in the eyes of fish, tears
行春を　近江の人と　惜しみける
yuku haru wo / Ōmi no hito to / oshimi keru
lamenting spring's passing / with the people of Ōmi — / how we mourned it
草臥て　宿かるころや　藤の花
kutabirete / yado karu koro ya / fuji no hana
weary, the hour / to find lodging — / wisteria in bloom
梅が香に　のつと日の出る　山路かな
ume ga ka ni / notto hi no deru / yamaji kana
into the plum's fragrance — / the sun rising suddenly: / a mountain road
傘に　押しわけみたる　柳かな
kasa ni / oshiwakemitaru / yanagi kana
pushing aside with my umbrella — / the willow
春雨や　蜂の巣つたふ　屋根の漏り
harusame ya / hachi no su tsutau / yane no mori
spring rain — / running down the wasp's nest, / the leaking roof
うぐひすや　竹の子藪に　老を鳴く
uguisu ya / takenoko yabu ni / oi wo naku
the warbler — / in the young-bamboo thicket, / singing of old age
Summer
六月や峯に雲置あらし山
roku-gatsu ya / mine ni kumo oku / arashi yama
the sixth month- / a cloud set on its peak / Mt. Storm
araumi ya / sado ni yokotau / Amanogawa
The rough sea— / Extending toward Sado Isle, / The Milky Way.
馬ぼくぼく我を絵に見る夏野かな
Uma bokuboku / ware wo e ni miru / natsu no kana
The horse, clop... clop... / I see myself in a picture, / summer fields.
島々や　千々に砕けて　夏の海
Shimajima ya / chijini kudakete / natsu no umi
Island after island — / into a myriad, crumbled, / the summer ocean.
掬ぶより　はや歯に　ひびく　泉かな
Musubu yori / haya ha ni hibiku / izumi kana
Just by scooping, / a sting to the teeth — / the spring.
さざれ蟹　足這いのぼる　清水かな
Sazare gani / ashi hai noboru / shimizu kana
A little crab / crawls up my foot — / pure water.
ひとつ脱でうしろに負いぬ　更衣
Hitetsu nuide / ushiro ni oi nu / koromogae
Took off a layer, / flung it on my back — / change of garment.
山を庭に　動き入るるや　夏座敷
Yama mo niwa ni / ugoki iruruya / natsu zashiki
Even the mountain / seems to move into the garden — / summer parlour.
風流の　初やおくの　田植うた
Furyu no / hajime ya oku no / taue uta
The source of sweet simplicity — / a north province's / rice-plant song.
皿鉢も　ほのかに闇の　宵涼み
Sara hachi mo / honoka ni yami no / yoi suzumi
Plates and bowls / faint, feel cool on / a dark evening.
吹く風の　中を魚　飛ぶ　御祓かな
Fuku kaze no / naka wo uo tobu / misogi kana
In the breeze / fish are jumping, / purification.
おもしろうて　やがて悲しき　鵜舟かな
Omoshiroute / yagate kanashiki / ubune kana
Fascinating, / and then, sorrowful, / cormorant boats.
灌仏の　皺手合はする　数珠の音
Kanbutsu no / shiwa te awasuru / juzu no oto
Bathed the Buddha — / wrinkled hands together, / rub the rosary.
あつしあつしと　門々の声
atsushi atsushi to / kadokado no koe
so hot, so hot — / voices at every gate
おもしろうて　やがて悲しき　鵜舟かな
omoshiroute / yagate kanashiki / ubune kana
delightful — / and soon after, sad: / the cormorant boats
あらたうと　青葉若葉の　日の光
ara tōto / aoba wakaba no / hi no hikari
how holy — / the light of the sun / on green leaves, young leaves
夏草や　兵共が　夢の跡
natsukusa ya / tsuwamono-domo ga / yume no ato
summer grasses — / all that remains / of warriors' dreams
さみだれを　あつめて早し　最上川
samidare wo / atsumete hayashi / Mogamigawa
gathering all / the rains of May — / swift, the Mogami
うき我を　さびしがらせよ　閑古鳥
uki ware wo / sabishigarase yo / kankodori
make me lonelier still, / o cuckoo, / wretched as i am
Autumn
灰うちたたく　宵の稲妻
hai uchitaku / yoi no inazuma
ashes beaten and scattered — / lightning at dusk
芭蕉野分して　望に雨を聞く夜かな
bashō nowaki shite / nozomi ni ame wo kiku yoru kana
the banana plant torn by storm — / a night of listening / to rain in a basin
野ざらしを　心に風の　しむ身かな
nozarashi wo / kokoro ni kaze no / shimu mi kana
resolved to be / a bleached skeleton — / the wind cuts to the bone
猿を聞く人　捨て子に秋の風いかに
saru wo kiku hito / sutego ni aki no / kaze ikani
you who hear the monkey cry — / what of the abandoned child / in the autumn wind?
吹きとばす　石はあさまの　野分かな
fukitobasu / ishi wa Asama no / nowaki kana
blowing away the stones — / Mt. Asama's / autumn gale
荒海や　佐渡によこたふ　天の河
araumi ya / Sado ni yokotau / amanogawa
the rough sea — / stretching toward Sado island, / the Milky Way
一家に　遊女も寝たり　萩と月
hitotsu ya ni / yūjo mo netari / hagi to tsuki
under one roof — / courtesans asleep too, / bush clover and moon
病雁の　夜寒に落ちて　旅寝かな
yamigarasu / yosamu ni ochite / tabine kana
a sick wild goose / falling in the cold night — / sleeping on the road
Winter
雪の朝　独り干鮭を　噛み得タリ
yuki no asa / hitori karazake wo / kami etari
a snowy morning — / alone, I manage / to gnaw dried salmon
鷹一つ　見付けてうれしい　いらこ崎
taka hitotsu / mitsukete ureshi / Irako-zaki
one hawk found — / what joy: / Cape Irako
初しぐれ　猿も小蓑を　ほしげなり
hatsushigure / saru mo komino wo / hoshige nari
the first winter rain — / the monkey too seems to want / a little raincoat
寒菊や　粉糠のかかる　臼の端
kangiku ya / konuka no kakaru / usu no hata
winter chrysanthemum — / rice bran dusted / on the edge of the mortar
New Year
甲比丹もくばはせけり君が春
kapitan mo / tsukuba hase keri / kimi ga haru
the Capatin also, / came to bow to; / our Lord's spring
