野々口立圃  ·  20 poems
Spring
かをり来て目に酢ぞたまる梅の花
kaori kite / me ni su zo tamaru / ume no hana
the scent wafts over / and its sour tang irritates my eyes / plum blossoms
源氏ならで上下に祝ふ若菜かな
genji narade / jōge ni iwau / wakana kana
it ain't Genji / but the halves & have nots are / celebrating “Young Herbs”
声なくて花や梢の高笑ひ
koe nakute / hana ya kozue no / taka-warai
without a voice, / the blossoms boughs ring / with high laughter
花ひとつたもとにすがる童かな
hana hitotsu / tamoto ni sugaru / warabe kana
one bough of blossoms / clinging to my sleeve sleeve / a child
ほころぶや尻も結ばぬ糸桜
hokorobu ya / shiri mo musubanu / ito-zakura
coming apart at the seems— / like a stitch with its end unfastened / thread cherry
天も花にゑへるか雲のみだれあし
ten mo hana ni / weeru ka kumo no / midareashi
even the sky / drunk on on blossoms? the clouds / stumble on wobbly legs
やあしばらく　花に酔へる　雲の乱れ足
yā shibaraku / hana ni yoeru / kumo no midarashi
wait — just a moment — / drunk on blossoms, / the cloud's staggering step
天も花に　酔へる　鐘撞く事
ten mo hana ni / yoeru / kane tsuku koto
even heaven drunk on blossoms — / the tolling of the bell
かをり来て　目に酔ひたまる　梅の花
kaori kite / me ni yoi tamaru / ume no hana
the fragrance drifts in — / pooling behind the eyes, / plum blossoms
Summer
松風の琴の唱歌や蝉のこゑ
matsukaze no / koto no shōga ya / semi no koe
pining wind’s / harp notes accompanied by / the cry of cicadas
颯々のすずしさや此の松の声
satsusatsu no / suzushisa ya kono / matsu no koe
murmuring / coolness— this / pining voice
篝火も蛍もひかる源氏かな
kagaribi mo / hotaru mo hikaru / genji kana
Cresset Fires and / Fireflies shining / Genji
Autumn
霧の海の底なる月はくらげ哉
kiri no umi no / sokonaru tsuki wa / kurage naka
at the bottom / of an ocean of fog, the moon, darkly / like a jellyfish
絵に似たる顔やヘマムシ夜半の月
e ni nitaru / kao ya hemamushi / yowa no tsuki
like a portrait / of a hemamushi face— / midnight moon
月影をくみこぼしけり手水鉢
tsukikage wo / kumi-koboshi keri / chōubachi
Scooping up the moon / In the wash-basin, / And spilling it.
月花の三句めをいま知る世かな
tsuki hana no / sanku-me wa ima / shiru yo kana
The moon, and the cherry-blossoms,— / Now I know, in this world, / What the third verse is!
散る音も色もさらさの紅葉哉
chiru oto mo / iro mo sarasa mo / momiji kana
sound of scattering / and hue as well, chintz calico / autumn leaves
Winter
あらはれて見えよ芭蕉の雪女
arawarete / mieyo bashō no / Yukionna
appear and / show yourself banana / snow woman
New Years
春永といふやことばのかざり縄
harunaga to / ufu ya kotoba no / kazari nawa
draping the verbal garland / of “the abiding spring” / like a sacred straw hawser
餅花をけふや宰府の神のぬさ
ten mo hana ni / weeru ka kumo no / midareashi
dumpling blossoms / will be, today, here in Dazai / my Shinto wand
