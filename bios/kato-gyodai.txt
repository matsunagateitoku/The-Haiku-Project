Katō Gyōdai (1732–1792) was a haiku poet and disciple of Yosa Buson, known for verse depicting mountain landscapes and rural life. Born in Echigo Province (present-day Niigata), he eventually moved to Kyoto to study under Buson and became one of his trusted followers. His haiku are admired for their clarity and the freshness of their natural imagery, reflecting his origins in a region of heavy snowfall and dramatic scenery.
