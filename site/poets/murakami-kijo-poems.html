Murakami Kijō
村上鬼城  ·  26 poems
Spring
己が影を慕ふて這へる地虫かな
ono ga kage wo / shitōte haeru / jimushi kana
Yearning towards / its own shadow, there creeps / a grab.
世を恋うて人をおそるる余寒かな
yo wo kōte / hito wo osoruru / jimushi kana
I long to go out, / yet I am fearful of people— / cold season lingers.
春の夜や灯を囲み居る盲者達
haru no yo ya / hi wo kakomi iru / mekura-tachi
Spring night: / sitting around a lamp, relax / blind men in a group.
しらしらと人踏まで暮るる落花かな
shira-shira to / hito fumade kururu / rakka kana
How white they are! / It darkens, no one trampling / the fallen blossoms.
春雨やたしかに見たる石の精
harusame ya / tashika ni mi-taru / ishi no sei
Spring rain: / I am certain I saw / the spirits of stones.
春寒やぶつかり歩く盲犬
haru-samu ya / butsukari aruku / mekura inu
Cold day in spring: / bumping into this and that, / a blind dog walking.
よき衣のつめたき裏や春浅き
yoki kinu no / tsumetaki ura ya / haru asashi
a fine kimono / how cool lining feels― / spring is shallow
Summer
夏草を這ひ上りたる捨子かな
natsu kusa wo / haiagari-taru / sutego kana
Crawling up and up / on a blade of summer grass / an abandoned silkworm.
さみだれや起き上がりたる根無し草
samidare ya / okiagari-taru / nenashi-gusa
Long rainy days: / a weed. cut at the root, / raises its head.
浮草や蜘蛛渡りゐて水平ら
ukikusa ya / kumo watari-ite / mizu taira
Floating duckweed: / a spider passes over it / the water, calm.
水泡の相依れば消ゆ蓮の花
suihō no / aiyoreba kiyu / hasu no hana
The moment two bubbles / are united, they both vanish. / A lotus blooms.
水すまし水に跳て水鉄の如し
mizu-samushi / mizu ni hanete mizu / tetsu no gotoshi
A water spider / bounces on the water, and / the water is like steel.
静かさに堪へでたにしの移りけり
shizukasa ni / taede tanishi no / utsuri-keri
Not able to bear / the stillness, a mud snail / has moved!
早乙女や　泥手にはさむ　額髪
Saotome ya / doro te ni hasamu / hitaigami
Rice-planting girl — / between muddy fingers, / a forelock. / Observances / Entering the Peak
Autumn
今朝秋や見入る鏡に親の顔
kesa aki ya / miiru kagami ni / oya no kao
First autumn morning: / the mirror I stare into / shows my father’s face.
小春日や石を嚙み居る赤蜻蛉
koharubi ya / ishi wo kami iru / akatonbo
Balmy autumn day: / on a stone, biting it, / a red dragonfly.
御仏のお顔しみや秋の雨
mihotoke no / okao no shimi ya / aki no ame
On the Buddha’s / august face some pockmarks show: / autumnal rain.
秋暑し草の実につくむら雀
aki atsushi / kusa no mi ni tsuku / mura-suzume
Hot autumn day— / swarming to the grass seed, / a flock of sparrows.
土くれをかかへて死ぬる螽かな
tsuchikure wo / kakaete shinuru / inago kana
Clutching a lump / of earth, it lies dying— / a grasshopper.
Winter
冬の日や前に塞がる己が影
fuyu no hi ya / mae ni fusagaru / ono ga kage
The winter sun: / blocking the way ahead of me, / my own shadow.
冬蜂の死にどころなく歩きかり
fuyu-bachi no / shinidokoro naku / aruki-keri
A winter hornet / without a place to die / staggers along.
冬川や小さき石に浪の花
fuyu kawa ya / chiisaki ishi ni / nami no kana
A winter stream: / abloom on a little stone, / blossoms of water.
Unclassified
gantan ya / fudoshi tatande / makuragami
It’s New Year’s morning! / A neatly folded loincloth / Above my pillow.
jirōshu no / you hodo mo naku / samenikeri
Before I could get drunk / On that sake that cures deafness / I was sober again.
hyakushō ni / hibari agatte / yo aketari
A skylark soars, / And for the farmers / The day has dawned.
winter
冬蜂の死にどころなく歩きけり
fuyu hachi no / shini dokoro naku / aruki keri
A bee in winter / without anywhere to die / goes wandering on
