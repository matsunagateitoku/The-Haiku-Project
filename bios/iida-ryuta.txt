Iida Ryūta (1920–2007) was a post-war haiku poet known for warmth, accessibility, and technical precision. Born in Yamanashi Prefecture, he was the son of Iida Dakotsu and studied haiku from an early age in that tradition. He eventually took over editorship of the journal Unmo, continuing his father's publication, and taught many younger poets. His long career and consistent output made him one of the most widely read haiku poets of the later twentieth century.
