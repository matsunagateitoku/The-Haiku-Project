Yamaguchi Seishi (1901–1994) was one of the major haiku poets of the twentieth century and among the most innovative in his choice of subjects. Born in Kyoto, he studied under Takahama Kyoshi and contributed to Hototogisu before developing his own direction. His most distinctive contribution was to bring industrial, urban, and technological subjects — factories, locomotives, machinery — into haiku without abandoning traditional form, widening the thematic range of the art considerably. His long life allowed him to remain a productive and respected figure across nearly seven decades of Japanese haiku history.
