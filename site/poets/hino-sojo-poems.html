Hino Sōjō
日野草城  ·  22 poems
Spring
春暁や人こそ知らね木々の雨
shungyō ya / hito koso shirane / kigi no ame
spring dawnー / rain on the trees that / on one knows
春の灯や女は持たぬのどぼとけ
haru no hi ya / onna wa motanu / nodobotoke
Spring evening’s lamplight: / a woman; she does not have / an Adam’s apple.
ものの種握ぎれば生命ひしめける
mono-dane wo / nigireba inochi / hishimekeru
Seeds of some sort一 / grasping them, I feel the jostle / of lives against my palm.
春暁や人こそ知らね木々の雨
shungyō ya / hito koso shirane / kigi no ame
Early spring dawn: / nobody else would know of / this rain on the trees.
Summer
ひと拗ねてものいはず白き薔薇となる
At a flash of lighting / they blinked their eyes一 / the leafless trees.
She sulks, / says nothing, and becomes / a white rose.
熱退きぬ空漠として黄の疲弊
netsu hikinu / kūbaku to shite / ki no tsukare
The fever is gone. / in the immeasurable void, / yellow weariness.
夏の闇高熱のわれ発光す
natsu no yami / kōnetsu no ware / hakkō-su
Dark summer night一 / sick and feverish, I / emit a gleam.
リラの香と気づきて昼寝さめにけり
rira no ka to / kizukite hirune / same ni keri
It’s the lilac’s scent一 / So I notice, and wake / From my noonday nap.
Autumn
朝寒や歯磨匂ふ妻の口
asa samu ya / hamigaki niou / tsuma no kuchi
The morning cold: / smelling of tooth power, / my wife’s mouth.
わが思ひ月かげとなり空に満つ
waga omoi / tsuki kage to nari / sora ni mitsu
My pondering / turns into the moonlight / filling the sky.
かいつぶりさびしくなればくぐりけり
kairsuburi / sabishiku nareba / kuguri-keri
The grebe / when it becomes lonely / dives into the water.
いなづまにまばたきしたる枯木達
inazuma ni / mabataki shi taru / kareki tachi
At a flash of lighting / they blinked their eyes一 / the leafless trees.
朝顔やおもひを遂げしごとしぼむ
asagao ya / omoi wo togeshi / goto shibomu
The morning-glory: / like fulfilled desire / it withers away.
十六夜や溲瓶かがやく縁の端
izayoi ya / shubin kagayaku / en no hashi
Moon sixteen nights old: / my urine glass glitters / at the veranda’s edge.
夜番柝を打つ月光にひび走る
yoban ki wo / utsu gekkō ni / hibi hashiru
A night watch / strikes his clappers一at the sound / cracks run up the moonlight.
妻が持つ薊の棘を手に感ず
tsuma ga / motsu azami no toge wo / te ni kanzu
My wife holds / a thistle一I feel its prickles / in my hand.
秋の道日かげに入りて日に出でて
aki no michi / hikage ni irite / hi ni idete
The autumn road / enters the shade一and emerges / into the sunshine.
Winter
凩や堕胎草煮たれども
kogarashi ya / oroshi-gusa ni wa / nitare domo
Wintry gusts: / abortion-herb is boiling / and yet … and yet …
眼をとぢてむかしの恋にあたたまる
me wo tojite / mukashi no koi ni / atatamaru
I close my eyes / and bask in the warmth of love / that is long past.
荒草の今は枯れつつ安らかに
arakusa no / ima wa kare tsutsu / yasurakani
The weeds / are now beginning to wither, / and in peace, too.
雨はれてふたたび寒し根深汁
ame harete / futatabi samushi / nebuke-jiru
the rain cleared / again it’s cold / green onion soup
no-season
見えぬ目の方の眼鏡の玉も拭く
mienu me no / hō no megane no / tama mo fuku
Its sight has been lost / and yet, for that eye also / I polish the eyeglass.
