Tomiyasu Fūsei
富安風生  ·  21 poems
Spring
岩つかむ鳶もよろめ東風強し
iwa tsukamu / tobi mo yorome / kochi tsuyoshi
clutching a boulder / the black-kite totters / in the strong east wind
花屑に口開く鯉や夏めきて
hana kuzu ni / kuchi aku koi ya / natsu-mekite
To the falling shreds / of blossoms a carp opens its mouth: / summer has come.
洋館の日本料理や松の花
yōkan no / nihon ryōri ya / matsu no hana
At a western-style house / Japanese dishes are served: / blossoms on a pine tree.
ガソリンの真赤き天馬春の雨
gasorin no / ma-akaki tenba / haru no ame
At the gas station / a bright red Pegasus— / spring rain.
一めんの落花の水に蛙の眼
ichimen no / rakka no mizu ni / kaeru no me
All over the water / fallen blossoms spread, and amongst them / a frog’s eyes.
Summer
蝶低し葵の花の低ければ
chō hikushi / aoi no hana no / hikukereba
Butterflies fly low / because the hollyhock / flowers are low.
ハンケチ振って別れも愉し少女等は
hankechi futte / wakare mo tanoshi / otome-ra wa
Waving handkerchiefs, / they even enjoy a parting: / the young maidens.
一茎の百合の全力一蕾に
ikkei no / yuri no zenryoku / ichirai ni
A lily stalk / concentration all its might / into one flower bud.
父のごとまた母のごと大夏木
chichi no goto / mata haha no goto / ō-natsuki
Like a father / and also like a mother, / The huge summer tree.
Autumn
朝寒や汽鑵車ぬくく顔を過ぐ
asazamu ya / kikansha nukuku / kao wo sugu
The morning cold: / a stream engine passes / warmly before my face.
向き定めて流れそめたる木の実かな
muki kimete / nagare sometaru / konomi kana
After deciding / which way to turn, a berry / begins to float down.
銀閣寺門前の田の案山子かな
ginkakuji / monzen no ta no / kakashi kana
Silver Pavilion― / in the paddy before its gate, / a scarecrow.
箱庭に病葉落ちて大いなり
hakoniwa ni / byōyō ochite / ōinari
Into the miniature / garden a sick leaf has fallen, / and how large it is!
よろこべばしきりに落つる木の実かな
yorokobeba / shikirini otsuru / ko no mi kana
As I rejoice / they fall time and time again / nuts from the tree.
樫の実を掃くばかりなり墓掃除
kashi no mi wo / haku bakari nari / haka sōji
Sweeping off the acorns / is all I need to do, when / cleaning the graves.
望月のふと歪みしと見しはいかに
mochizuki no / futo yugamishi to / mishi wa ikani
The full moon, for / a moment, looked misshapen / and I wonder why.
本読めば本の中より虫の声
hon yomeba / hon no naka yori / mushi no koe
I read a book— / somewhere within the book / An insect chirps.
多勢の眼に一片の散紅葉
ōnei no / me ni hitohira no / chiri momiji
Before a great many / people’s eyes a solitary / tinted leaf falls.
よろぼへる蛾も林中の閑かさに
yoroboeru ga / mo rinchū no / shizukasa ni
It totters too— / a moth, under the stillness / of the grove.
満月を生みし湖山の息づかひ
mangetsu wo / umishi kozan no / ikizukai
After giving birth / to a full moon, lakeside mountains / breathe in and out.
Winter
大火事に明るき空や手水鉢
ōkaji ni / akaruki sora ya / chōzubachi
Under the sky, / brightened by a large fire, / a washing basin.
