Sugita Hisajo (1890–1946) was a haiku poet whose career combined artistic brilliance with personal struggle. Born in Kagoshima, she became a disciple of Takahama Kyoshi and published extensively in Hototogisu, becoming one of its most prominent contributors. Her haiku — sensuous, emotionally direct, and often focused on a woman's inner life — were acclaimed by readers but eventually led to a celebrated rupture with Kyoshi, who expelled her from his circle in 1936 over what he considered unseemly personal content in her verse. She spent her final decade in poverty and declining mental health. She is now recognized as one of the major female haiku poets of the twentieth century.
