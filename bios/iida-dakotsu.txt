Iida Dakotsu (1885–1962) was a major haiku poet of the Taishō and Shōwa eras, known for haiku of quiet depth rooted in nature and personal experience. Born in Yamanashi Prefecture, near the Japanese Alps, he composed haiku in association with the Hototogisu circle before founding his own journal, Unmo, through which he developed a more personal voice. His haiku are distinguished by attentiveness to the natural world and a melancholy strain that drew on his own experiences of illness and loss. The mountain landscapes of his native region are a recurring presence in his work.
