Katō Shūson (1905–1993) was a major haiku poet of the post-war era, known for a humanist vision that brought psychological depth and social consciousness to traditional form. Born in Tokyo, he became one of the leading figures of the "human exploration" school, which placed human experience at the center of haiku rather than treating people as incidental to the natural world. His poetry is notable for its emotional directness and willingness to address suffering and struggle. He taught at Meiji University and influenced a generation of post-war haiku poets.
