Kaneko Tōta (1919–2018) was one of the most influential and longest-lived haiku poets of the twentieth century. Born in Gunma Prefecture, he studied under Yamaguchi Seishi and developed a haiku practice that challenged seasonal conventions while retaining formal structure. He was a prolific critic as well as a poet, arguing throughout his career for a haiku that engaged with contemporary social life and resisted the nostalgic conservatism of the mainstream. He continued writing and publishing into his nineties, and his long career spanned the full arc of post-war Japanese haiku.
