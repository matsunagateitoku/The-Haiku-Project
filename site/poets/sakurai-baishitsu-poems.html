Sakurai Baishitsu
桜井梅室  ·  8 poems
Spring
寝勝手のよさに又見る柳かな
nekatte no / yoki ni mata miru / yanagi kana
turning to get comfortable / for my nap, again I see / the willow:
Summer
けしの散る光ひまなし枕元
keshi no chiru / hikaru hima nashi / makura moto
the scattering poppy— / its light so busy / at my pillow
Autumn
名月や草木に劣る人のかげ
meigetsu ya / kusa ki ni otoru / hito no kage
harvest moon— / the shadows of people / inferior to grass and trees
門ありて国分寺はなし草の花
mon arite / kokubun ji wa nashi / kusa no hana
the gate remains / but the temple is gone— / grass flowers
New year
元日や人の妻子の美しき
ganjitsu ya / hito no tsumako no / utsukushiki
New Year’s Day— / the neighborhood wives / looks so lovely
summer
桐の木や雨のながるる蝉の腹
kiri no ki ya / ame no ngaruru / semi no hara
paulownia tree— / the flowing rainwater / on the cicada's belly
winter
糊の干ぬ行灯ともす寒さかな
nori no hinu / andon tomosu / samusa kana
the paste not yet dry / and lighting the paper lamp— / the cold
ふゆの夜や針うしなうておそろしき
fuyu no yo ya / hari ushinaute / Osoroshiki
on a winter's night / how frightful it is / to drop a sewing needle
