Ōshima Ryōta (1718–1787) was one of the dominant haiku figures in Edo during the second half of the eighteenth century. He ran a large and influential school in the Yanaka district of Edo and attracted thousands of students throughout his career. His haiku are accessible, warm, and rooted in the natural world, continuing the classical tradition without the experimental ambitions of his contemporary Buson. He is less read today than Buson or Issa, but his school shaped the practice of a large number of poets in the late Edo period.
