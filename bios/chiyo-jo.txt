Chiyo-jo (1703–1775) — also known as Kaga no Chiyo, Fukuda Chiyo, and in her later years as Chiyo-ni — is the most celebrated female haiku poet of the classical era. Born in Komatsu, Kaga Province (present-day Ishikawa Prefecture), she showed poetic talent from an early age and studied under local masters. Her most famous poem — asking her neighbors for water because morning glories have wound around her well bucket — became one of the most quoted haiku in Japanese literary history. She took Buddhist vows in 1754 and thereafter signed her work Chiyo-ni. She remained a prolific composer throughout her life, and her poems circulated widely across Japan during her lifetime, an unusual achievement for a woman poet of her era.
