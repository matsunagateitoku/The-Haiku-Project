Murakami Kijō (1865–1938) was one of the earliest modern haiku poets and an important figure in the reform movement associated with Masaoka Shiki. Born in Nagasaki, he later moved to Osaka, where he became a friend and associate of Shiki and contributed to the early development of modern haiku. His poems are traditional in form but reflect the new emphasis on direct observation that Shiki advocated. He is one of the transitional figures between the late Edo-period haiku tradition and the modern movement.
