Naitō Jōsō (1662–1704) was a Buddhist monk and one of Bashō's most trusted disciples. Born into a samurai family, he entered monastic life and eventually joined Bashō's circle in Edo, accompanying the master on parts of his later journeys and remaining close until Bashō's death in Osaka in 1694. His haiku are known for their spare elegance and quiet Buddhist atmosphere. Bashō himself placed Jōsō among the best of his followers, and his work has retained the respect of scholars even as it remains less widely read than that of Kikaku or Kyorai.
