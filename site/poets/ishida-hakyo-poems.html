Ishida Hakyō
石田波郷  ·  23 poems
Spring
立春の米こぼれをり葛西橋
risshun no / kome kobore-ori / kasai-bashi
beginning of spring / rice is spilt / on the Kasai Bridge
花冷の顔ばかりなり雲の中
hana-bie no / kao bakari nari / kumo no naka
blossom chill / they are only faces / in clouds
さくらの芽のはげしさ仰ぎ蹌めける
sakura no me / no hageshisa aogi / yoromekeru
In the cherry tree’s buds / an intense force; looking upward / at them, I stagger.
蝶死にて流るる水を今も踰ゆ
chō shinite / nagaruru mizu wo / ima mo koyu
The butterfly, now dead, / floats across the flowing water / once again.
春嵐屍は敢えて出でゆくも
haru arashi / kabane wa aete / ide yuku mo
Spring storm— / a corpse courageously / leaves the hospital.
Summer
蟬の朝愛憎は悉く我に還る
semi no asa / aizō wa kotogotoshiku / ware ni kaeru
Cicadas’ morning— / all my loves and hatreds / have come back to me.
栗咲く香血を喀く前もその後も
kuri saku ka / chi wo haku mae mo / sono ato mo
Chestnut blossoms were fragrant / before I vomited blood— / and they are still.
泉への道後れゆく安けさよ
izumi e no / michi okure yuku / yasukesa yo
On the way towards / the fountain I lag behind, / and how calm I feel!
Autumn
檻の鷲さびしくなれば羽つかも
ori no washi / sabishiku nareba / ha utsu mako
The caged eagle, / beginning to feel lonely, / flaps his wings.
秋の暮業火となりて秬は燃ゆ
aki no kure / gōka to narite / kibi wa moyu
Nightfall in autumn— / turning into flames of hellfire, / cornhusks burn.
朝寒の市電兵馬と別れたり
asa samu no / shiden heiba to / wakaretari
In the morning cold / a streetcar begins to part / from soldiers and horses.
朝顔の紺の彼方の月日かな
asagao no / kon no kanata no / tsukihi kana
The morning-glory— / far beyond its dark blue, / months and days to come.
稲妻す妻の来し方我行く方
inazuma su / tsuma no koshi kata / waga yuku kata
Lightning flashes / where my wife has come from, / whither I go.
蛼赤しほのぼの熱の上るとき
kōrogi akashi / honobono netsu no / agaru toki
A red cricket. / My fever, glimmering, / reaches a peak.
麻薬うてば十三夜月遁走す
mayaku uteba / jūsanya-zuki / tonsōsu
As the drug takes hold, / the moon of the thirteenth night / scampers away from me.
起ち上らざるもの胸に萩起す
tachiagarazaru / mono mune ni / hagi okosu
With something in my heart / that will not stand up, I help / the bush clover to stand up.
Winter
霜の道馬糞その他をうべなへり
shimo no michi / bafun sono ta wo / ubenari
On the frosty road / horse dung lies—I accept it, / and everything else.
貨車寒し百千の墓うちふるひ
kasha samushi / hyaku sen no haka / uchifurui
Freight trains, cold. / Hundreds and thousands of tombstones / trembling with them.
焼跡に透きとほりけり寒の水
yakeato ni / sukitōri keri / kan no mizu
Amid the debris / perfectly transparent / midwinter water.
力なく降る雪なればなぐさまず
chikara naku / furu yuki nareba / nagusamazu
So languidly / snowflakes fall, offering no / consolation to me.
雪はしづかにゆたかのはやし屍室
yuki wa shizukani / yutakani hayashi / kabaneshitsu
It snows quietly, / abundantly, and fast— / a mortuary.
百千の土管口あけ雪降れり
hyaku sen no / dokan kuchi ake / yuki fureri
Hundreds and thousands / of earthen pipes, with their mouths / open, the snow falls.
白き手の病者ばかり落葉焚
shiroki te no / byōsha bakari / ochiba-taki
all whites hands of / ill patiants / above the pile of burning leaves
