Spring
石塔もはや苔つくや春の雨
sekitō / mohaya koke tsuku ya / haru no ame
on the stone stupa / already moss grows― / spring rain
花守や白き頭をつきあわせて
Hana mori ya blossom guards- / Shiroki kashira wo poking white heads together / Tsukiawasete for a chat
the blossom keeper— / two white heads / bent together
さほひめやかくる柳の長かもじ
saho hime ya / kakuru yanagi no / naga kamoji
Saho-hime! / the willow conceals her / long streaming hair
鶯が引琴の緒や糸柳
uguisu ga / hiki koto no o ya / itoyanagi
the warbler strums / the koto strings— / the thread willow
春雨にあらいてけづれ柳がみ
harusame ni / araite kezure / yanagikami
washed in spring rain / and combed through— / willow hair
もえながら有るや火鼠のかは柳
moe nagara / aru ya hinezumi no / kawa yanagi
burning yet surviving— / the fire-rat pelt / and riverside willow
気力なしと誰かは見ましこぶ柳
kiryoku nashi to / dare ka wa mimashi / kobu yanagi
lacking all vigor— / who would see that / in a gnarled willow?
観音のちからや見するこぶ柳
Kannon no / chikara ya misuru / kobu yanagi
by Kannon's power— / revealed to us: / the gnarled willow
鉢たたき来ぬ夜となれば朧なり
hachi tataki / konu yo to nareba / oboro nari
the bowl-beaters gone / and night grown hazy— / the bowl-striking night
うごくとも見えで畑打つ男かな
ugoku to mo / miede hata utsu / otoko kana
seeming not to move / yet plowing the fields— / the man at work
花守や白き頭をつき合せ
hanamori ya / shiroki atama wo / tsuki awase
blossom keepers— / their white heads / bumping together
何事ぞ花見る人の長刀
nanigoto zo / hanami ru hito no / naginata
what's this all about? / the long sword / of the blossom-viewer
一昨日（おととひ）はあの山こえつ花ざかり
ototoi wa / ano yama koetsu / hanazakari
the day before yesterday / I crossed that mountain— / blossoms at their peak
Summer
郭公（ほととぎす）なくや雲雀と十文字
hototogisu / naku ya hibari to / jumonji
the cuckoo cries— / crossing paths with the lark / in a cross shape
うの花の絶間たゝかん闇の門
uno hana no / tatema tatakan / yami no kado
in the gap of deutzia / I shall knock— / the gate in darkness
湖の水まさりけり五月雨
mizu no umi / masari keri / samidare
the lake water / has risen— / the long summer rains
手のうへにかなしく消ゆる螢かな
te no ue ni / kanashiku kiyuru / hotaru kana
on the palm of my hand / sadly fading out— / a firefly
螢火や吹きとばされて鳰のやみ
hotaruhi ya / fuki tobasarete / niwa no yami
firefly light— / blown away / into the garden's dark
石も木も眼にひかる暑さかな
ishi mo ki mo / me ni hikaru / atsusa kana
stones and trees alike / glittering to the eye— / the heat
Autumn
魂棚の奥なつかしや親の顔
tamadana no / oku natsukashi ya / ota no kao
in the depths of the spirit shelf— / how dear to me: / father's face
いなづまやどの傾城とかり枕
inazuma ya / dono keisei to / kari makura
lightning flash— / which courtesan / borrowed pillow?
稲妻のかきまぜて行くやみよかな
inazuma no / kakimazete yuku / yami yo kana
the lightning stirs / and stirs as it passes— / the dark night
岩端や爰（ここ）にもひとり月の客
iwa hana ya / koko ni mo hitori / tsuki no kyaku
ledge of rock— / here too is one / guest of the moon
あき風やしら木の弓に弦はらん
aki kaze ya / shira ki no yumi ni / tsuru haran
autumn wind— / I shall string the bow / of pale birch wood
雁がねの竿になる時尚さびし
karigane no / sao to naru toki / nao sabishi
when the wild geese / form their pole formation— / still more lonely
故郷も今はかり寝や渡鳥
furusato mo / ima wa kari ne ya / watari dori
even home / is now a borrowed sleep— / the migrating bird
二番草　取りも果たさず　穂に出でて
nibangusa / tori mo hatasazu / ho ni idete
the second weeding / not yet finished — / ears of grain appear
Winter
鳶の羽もかいつくろひぬ初しぐれ
tobi no hane mo / kaitsu kuroi nu / hatsushigure
even the kite's wings / have been preened and smoothed— / the season's first rain
いそがしや沖の時雨の眞帆片帆
isogashi ya / oki no shigure no / maho kataho
so busy: / the winter squall at sea / full sail, half sail
こがらしの地にも落さぬしぐれかな
kogarashi no / chi ni mo otosanu / shigure kana
the wintry blast— / not even letting it fall to earth, / this winter rain
鴨啼くや弓矢を捨てて十余年
kamo naku ya / yumiya wo sute te / juyo nen
ducks are calling— / been ten-odd years / since I laid down bow and arrow
荒磯やはしり馴れたる友千鳥
araiso ya / hashiri nare taru / tomo chidori
the rocky shore— / accustomed to running there, / flocks of plovers
応々といへどたゝくや雪の門
o-o to / iedo tataku ya / yuki no kado
"Yes, yes," I call out— / but they keep knocking / at the snow-covered gate
尾頭の心もとなき海鼠かな
odono no / kokoromotonaki / namako kana
head and tail / both uncertain— / the sea cucumber
木枯の地にも落とさぬ　しぐれかな
kogarashi no / chi ni mo otosanu / shigure kana
the winter gale / doesn't let it reach the ground — / the drizzle
おうおうと　いへどもうごかぬ　雪の門
ōō to / iedomo ugokanu / yuki no kado
hey! hey! i call — / but it doesn't move: / the snowy gate
木枕の　垢や伊吹に　残る雪
kimakura no / aka ya Ibuki ni / nokoru yuki
grime on the wooden pillow — / and on Mt. Ibuki, / lingering snow
New Year
元日や家に譲りの太刀帯かん
ganjitsu ya ie ni yuzuri no tachi hakan
first day- / the sword past down in my family / I will wear
元日や家に譲りの太刀帯（は）かん
ganjitsu ya / ie ni yuzuri no / tachi hakan
New Year's morning— / my inherited sword / worn at the waist
Unclassified
ただとひやうしに　長き脇指
tada to hyōshi ni / nagaki wakizashi
drawn by chance alone — / a long short sword
