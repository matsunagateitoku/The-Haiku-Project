Arakida Moritake (1473–1549) was a Shinto priest at the Outer Shrine of Ise (Geku) and one of the earliest major figures in the haiku tradition. A skilled practitioner of classical renga, he turned his attention to haikai and is credited with some of the first haiku of lasting literary quality. His most famous verse compares a fallen flower flying back to its branch with a butterfly — an image that became a touchstone of early haiku aesthetics. He reportedly composed around twenty thousand verses over his lifetime and compiled them in the collection Dokugin ichiman-ku.
