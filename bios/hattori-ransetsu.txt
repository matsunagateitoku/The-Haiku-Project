Hattori Ransetsu (1654–1707) was one of Bashō's ten principal disciples and the figure most responsible for continuing Bashō's sabi style in Edo after the master's death. Born Hattori Hatsutarō, he became one of Bashō's earliest Edo students and remained faithful to his teacher's austere aesthetic throughout his career. His poems are quiet and restrained, lacking the pyrotechnics of Kikaku but maintaining the spirit of Bashō's mature work. He died in the same year as Kikaku, 1707, leaving a body of verse that kept Bashō's influence alive in the generation immediately following his death.
