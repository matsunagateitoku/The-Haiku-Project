Kagami Shikō (1665–1731) was one of Bashō's disciples and one of the principal custodians of his teacher's reputation after 1694. He studied directly under Bashō and became part of the inner circle, though a more peripheral figure than Kyorai or Kikaku. After Bashō's death, Shikō compiled materials about his master's teachings and worked to transmit his style to students in the provinces. His editorial role was important but controversial — other disciples accused him of misrepresenting Bashō's views in his compilations, and disputes over his reliability shadowed his posthumous reputation.
