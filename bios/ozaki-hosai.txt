Ozaki Hōsai (1885–1926) was a haiku poet whose life followed the trajectory of social failure, religious withdrawal, and creative freedom. Born Ozaki Hideo in Tottori Prefecture, he built a career as an insurance company executive while writing haiku in his spare time. Personal crises — depression, divorce, alcoholism — eventually led him to abandon his career, take Buddhist orders, and live as a caretaker at remote temples. His free-verse haiku, written in the last years of his life on Shōdo Island in the Inland Sea, are among the most spare and candid in the tradition. He died on Shōdo Island in 1926 at the age of forty-one.
