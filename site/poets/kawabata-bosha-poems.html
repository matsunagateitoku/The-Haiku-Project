Kawabata Bosha
川端茅舎  ·  20 poems
Spring
蝶の空七堂伽藍さかしまに
chō no sora / shichidō-garan / sakashima ni
To the butterfly in the sky / all buildings on the temple ground / are upside down.
春の夜や寝れば恋しき観世音
haru no yo ya / nereba koishiki / kanzeon
Nightfall on a spring day: / in bed, I think longingly / of the gentle Bodhisattva.
葱の花ふと金色の仏かな
negi no hana / futo konjiki no / hotoke kana
Green onions flowering— / for a moment golden / Buddha was there.
Summer
水晶の念珠に映る若葉かな
suishō no / nenju ni utsuru / wakaba kana
In the crystal beads / of my rosary young leaves / are mirrored.
まひまひや雨後の円光とりもどし
maimai ya / ugo no enkō / torimodoshi
Now that the shower / has passed, the water spider / regains its halo.
まひまひの水輪に鐘の響かな
maimai no / minawa ni kane no / hibiki kana
The water spider / draws rings on the water, wherein / echoes a temple bell.
光輪を負ひて貧しき田螺かな
kōrin wo / oite mazushiki / tanishi kana
Carrying a halo / on its back, how impoverished / a mud snail!
緑陰に黒猫の目のかつと金
ryokuin ni / kuroneko no me no / katto kin
In the shade of green / leaves the eyes of a black cat / glaring in gold.
我が魂のごとく朴咲き病よし
waga tama no / gotoku hō saki / yamai yoshi
As if it were my soul / a magnolia blooms out— / ailing, I feel better.
Autumn
院々の肉煮ゆる香や夕紅葉
in’in no / niku niyuru ka ya / yū momiji
From the monks’ cells / a smell of cooking meat: / autumn leaves at nightfall.
金剛の露ひとつぶや石の上
kongō no / tsuyu hitotsubu ya / ishi no ue
Like a diamond / a drop of dew, all alone / on a stone.
虎杖を啣へて沙弥や墓掃除
itadori wo / kuwaete shami ya / haka sōji
A stem of knotweed / in his mouth, a young acolyte / sweeps the graveyard.
僧酔うて友の頭撫づる月の縁
sō yōte / tomo no zu nazuru / tsuki no en
The monk, a little / drunk, pats a friend on the head— / terrace in the moonlight.
眼前に蜘の巣かかり夕山河
ganzen ni / kumo no su kakari / yū sanga
A spider web / hanging before my eyes, evening / mountains and rivers.
石枕してわれ蟬か泣き時雨
ishi makura / shite ware semi ka / naki shigure
My head on a pillow / of stone, am I a cicada? / The weeping of rain...
Winter
大根馬菩薩面して眼になみだ
daikon uma / bosatsu-zura shite / me ni namida
The horse loaded with turnips / has a Bodhisattva’s face, / with tears in his eyes.
月の雪あをあを闇を染めのけり
tsuki no yuki / aoao yami wo / somenokeri
Snow under the moon / is blue, dyeing with its blue / the darkness of night.
氷る夜の文殊に燭をたてまつる
kōru yo no / monju ni shoku wo / tatematsuru
On a freezing night / to the Bodhisattva of Wisdom / I offer a candle.
月光に深雪の創のかくれなし
gekkō ni / miyuki no kizu no / kakure nashi
In the moonlight / how clearly the deep snow / shows its scars!
火の玉の如くに咳きて隠れ栖む
hi no tama no / gotoku ni sekite / kakure sumu
Like a fireball, I / fall into a fit of coughing / in this hideout of mine.
