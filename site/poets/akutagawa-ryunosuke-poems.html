Akutagawa Ryūnosuke
芥川龍之介  ·  21 poems
Spring
熱を病んで桜明りに震へゐる
netsu wo yande / sakura akari ni / furue iru
Sick and feverish / in the gleam of cherry blossoms / I keep shivering.
うららかに毛虫わたるや松の枝
uraranani / kemushi wataru ya / matsu no eda
Under the bright sky / a hairy caterpillar crawls / on the pine tree’s branch.
Summer
蝶の舌ゼンマイに似る暑さかな
chō no shita / zenmai ni niru / atsusa kana
The butterfly’s tongue / resembles a steel spring— / what a hot day!
青蛙おのれもペンキ塗りたてか
aogaeru / onoree mo penki / nuritate ka
Green frog, / have you also had your body / freshly painted?
蟻地獄陰して牡丹花赤き
arijigoku / kage shite botan / hana akaki
An ant lion’s pit / is hidden underneath a peony / blossoming in red.
赤百合の蕋黒む暑さ極りぬ
akayuri no / shibe kuromu atsusa / kiwamarinu
The red lily’s pistil / darkens as the hot spell / reaches its peak.
炎天に上りて消える箕の埃
enten ni / noborite kieru / mi no hokori
The the sweltering sky / it rises and disappears: / dust from the winnowing.
石の垢ものうき水の目ざしかな
ishi no aka / monouki mizu no / hizashi kana
Slime on the stones— / gloomily through the water / the rays of the sun.
木の枝の瓦にさはる暑さかな
ki no eda no / kawara ni sawaru / atsusa kana
Branches of a tree / touching the tiles of the roof— / how hot it is!
静かさに堪えず散りけり夏椿
shizukasa ni / taezu chiri-keri / natsu tsubaki
Unable to stand / with the stillness, it falls— / summer camellia.
兎も片耳垂るる大暑かな
usagi mo / kata-mimi taruru / taisho kana
Even the rabbit / droops one of her ears— / midsummer heat!
Autumn
秋立つ日うろ歯に銀をうづめけり
aki tatsu hi / uroba ni gin wo / uzume-keri
The day autumn began / I had a cavity in my tooth / filled with silver.
白菊や匂にもある影日なた
shiragiku ya / nioi ni mo aru / kage hinata
White chrysanthemums: / in the fragrance, too, there are / light and shade.
山の月冴えて落葉の匂かな
yama no tsuki / saete ochiba no / nioi kana
The moon above the hill / is serene; under it the smell / of fallen leaves.
秋風や黒子に生えし毛一根
akikaze ya / hokuro ni haeshi / ke ikkon
The wind of autumn: / a hair has begun to grow / on my mole.
松風や人は月下に松露を掘る
matsukaze ya / hito wa gekka ni / shōro wo horu
Wind through the pine trees: / in the moonlight someone / digs truffles.
初秋の蝗つかめば柔かき
hatsu aki no / inago tsukameba / yawarakaki
Early autumn— / as I grab a grasshopper, / how soft it feels!
秋風や甲羅をあます膳の蟹
akikaze ya / kōra wo amasu / zen no kani
The wind of autumn: / only the carapace remains / of a crab on the tray.
Winter
木がらしや目刺にのこる海のいろ
kogarashi ya / mezashi ni nokoru / umi no iro
Wintry gusts: / on the sardine still lingers / the ocean’s colour.
水洟や鼻の先だけ暮れ残る
mizubana ya / hana no saki dake / kure nokoru
My runny nose: / everywhere, except on that spot, / evening dusk falls.
New Years
元日や手を洗ひをる夕ごころ
ganjitsu ya / te wo arai-oru / yū-gokoro
New Years Day / washing my hands / evening heart
