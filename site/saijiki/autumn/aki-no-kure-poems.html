秋の暮  ·  5 poems
我たつるけむりは人の秋の暮
ware tatsu ru / kemuri wa hito no / aki no kure
the smoke I raise is another person's autumn dusk
Narita Sokyu
泣きやまぬ子に灯ともすや秋の暮
naki yamanu / ko ni hi tomosu ya / aki no kure
For a baby who keeps crying she lights a lamp: evening in autumn.
Kawahigashi Hekigotō
秋の暮大魚の骨を海が引く
aki no kure / taigyo no hone wo / umi ga hiku
Autumn nightfall— the skeleton of a huge fish is drawn out to the sea.
Saitō Sanki
秋の暮水中もまた暗くなる
aki no kure / suichū mo mata / kuraku naru
Evening in autumn一 under the water also it becomes dark.
Yagichi Seishi
秋の暮業火となりて秬は燃ゆ
aki no kure / gōka to narite / kibi wa moyu
Nightfall in autumn— turning into flames of hellfire, cornhusks burn.
Ishida Hakyō
