Mukai Kyorai (1651–1704) was among the most trusted of Bashō's disciples and the keeper of the master's teachings after his death. Born in Nagasaki, the son of a physician, he eventually settled in Kyoto and became one of Bashō's inner circle. He owned the Rakushisha (Fallen Persimmon Cottage) in Saga, Kyoto, where Bashō stayed during visits to the city. With Nozawa Bonchō, he co-edited the anthology Sarumino (1691). His most enduring contribution is the Kyoraishō, a record of Bashō's conversations and aesthetic judgments compiled over several years — the most important single document of Bashō's poetic theory.
