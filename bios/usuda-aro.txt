Usuda Arō (1879–1951) was a haiku poet and associate of the reform movement in early twentieth century haiku. He studied under Kawahigashi Hekigodō and contributed to the debate over the direction of modern haiku, leaning toward a looser, more observational approach than the conservative Hototogisu school. He is a secondary figure in the broader history of haiku but is associated with the effort to bring greater immediacy and flexibility to the form.
