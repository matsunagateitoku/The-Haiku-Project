Akutagawa Ryūnosuke (1892–1927) is the central figure of Japanese modernist short fiction — the author of Rashōmon, In a Grove, Hell Screen, and dozens of other stories noted for their psychological acuity and literary sophistication. He was also a serious haiku poet throughout his life, publishing verse alongside his fiction. His haiku tend toward the lucid and spare, occasionally shading into the darker registers of his stories. He died by suicide in Tokyo in 1927 at the age of thirty-five, leaving a note describing "a vague anxiety about the future." The Akutagawa Prize, Japan's most prestigious literary award for new fiction, was established in his memory in 1935.
