Tagami Kikusha (1753–1826) was a female haiku poet who, unusually for a woman of her era, traveled Japan on foot in the manner of Bashō. Born in Nagato Province (present-day Yamaguchi Prefecture), she took Buddhist vows and spent years journeying through the country, composing haiku as she went. Her poems reflect the landscapes she passed through and a Buddhist sensibility cultivated in travel. She is one of the more remarkable female poets of the late Edo period.
