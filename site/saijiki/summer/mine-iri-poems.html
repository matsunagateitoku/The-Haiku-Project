峯入 Mine iri

貝吹いて　雪踏み分けよ　花の跡
Kai fuite / yuki fumi wake yo / hana no ato
Blow the conch shell, make way through snow! After the flowers.
Seira
峯入やおもへば深き　芳野山
Mine iri ya / omoeba fukaki / Yoshino yama
Entering the peak — when you think of it, Yoshino mountain is deep.
Shirao
峯入や　鳥さへ知らぬ　花を今日
Mine iri ya / tori sae shiranu / hana wo kyo
Entering the peak — today, flowers even birds don't know of.
Baisha
峯入の　老いてそのこる　美男眉
Mine iri no / oite mo nokoru / biman mayyu
He enters the peak — aging, but still handsome eyebrows.
Oikawa Tei
灌仏の　皺手合はする　数珠の音
Kanbutsu no / shiwa te awasuru / juzu no oto
Bathed the Buddha — wrinkled hands together, rub the rosary.
Basho
山寺や　五色にあまる　花み堂
Yamadera ya / goshiki ni amaru / hana mido
Mountain temple — more than five colours, the flowery pavilion.

灌仏の　御指の先や　暮の月
Kanbutsu no / oyubi no saki ya / kure no tsuki
At the bathed Buddha's august finger-tip, the dusk moon.
