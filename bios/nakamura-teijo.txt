Nakamura Teijo (1900–1988) was one of the major female haiku poets of the twentieth century and a long-serving figure in the traditional haiku world. Born in Kumamoto, she became a disciple of Takahama Kyoshi and contributed to Hototogisu. She later founded the journal Hana-tsubaki, through which she taught and published other poets for decades. Her haiku are known for their quiet precision and their faithful rendering of domestic and natural life. She remained active as a poet and teacher well into her eighties.
