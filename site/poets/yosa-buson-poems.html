Spring
公達に狐化けたり宵の春
kindachi ni / kitsune bake tari / yoi no haru
into a young nobleman, / the fox has transformed himself / on a spring evening
筋違にふとん敷きたり宵の春
sujikai ni / futon shiki tari / yoi no haru
at a slant, / the bedding is laid out / on a spring evening
のうれんに東風吹くいせの出店かな
nōren ni / kochi fuku ise no / dedana kana
through the curtains / the east wind blows, Ise / shop-stalls
春雨や小磯の小貝ぬるるほど
harusame ya / koiso no kogai / nururu hodo
spring rain― / until little shells on the beach / are moist
折り釘に烏帽子かけたり春の宿
ori kugi ni e / boshi kake tari / haru no yado
on a bent nail / a court-cap hangs; / a lodging in spring
指南車を胡地に引去る霞かな
shinansha o / kochi ni hikisaru / kasumi kana
the guide-cart of heaven / dragged off to the northern lands / beyond the haze
古庭に　鶯略き　ぬ日もすがら
furuniwa ni / uguisu naki / nu hi mo sugara
in the old garden — / the warbler cries / all through a rainy day
花守の　身は弓　矢なき　かしかな
hanamori no / mi wa yumi / yaki / kashi kana
the blossom-guardian's body — / bow without arrow, / oak tree
しら梅に　明る夜ばかりと　なりにけり
shirame ni / akuru yo bakari to / nari ni keri
the white plum — / it has become a night / brightened only by this
春雨や　小磯の小貝　ぬるるほど
harusame ya / koiso no kogai / nururu hodo
spring rain — / enough to wet / the tiny shells on the small shore
菜の花や　月は東に　日は西に
na no hana ya / tsuki wa higashi ni / hi wa nishi ni
rapeseed blossoms — / the moon in the east, / the sun in the west
Summer
にほひある衣も畳まず春の暮
nioi aru / kinu mo tatamazu / haru no kure
the perfumed / robe, left unfolded; / spring dusk
夏川を越すうれしさよ　手に草履
Natsu gawa wo / kosu ureshisa yo / te ni zori
How joyful to cross / a summer river! / Sandals in hand.
山々を低く覚ゆる　青田かな
Yama yama wo / hikuku oboyuru / aota kana
All the mountains / seem lower, by / green ricefields.
更衣　野路の人はつかに白し
Koromogae / noji no hito wazu / kani shireshi
Change of garments — / on field paths people / look whiter.
羅に　遮る蓮の　にほひかな
Usumono ni / saegiru hasu no / nioi kana
By a thin silk / kimono screened, the / scent of lotus.
細脛に夕風さはる　たかむしろ
Hoso hagi ni / yu kaze sawaru / takamushiro
On the thin legs / a dusk breeze — / bamboo mat.
後家の君　たそがれ顔の　うちはかな
Goke no kimi / tasogare gao no / uchiwa kana
The widow — / her face at dusk, behind / a round fan.
風鈴や　花にはつらき風ながら
Furin ya / hana seiun tsuraki / kaze nagare
Windbells — / though for flowers the / wind was cruel.
夕風や　水青鷺の　脛をうつ
yūkaze ya / mizu ao-sagi no / hagi wo utsu
the evening breeze — / water striking the legs / of the grey heron
さみだれや　大河を前に　家二軒
samidare ya / taiga wo mae ni / ie ni ken
the May rains — / facing the great river, / two houses
方百里　雨雲よせぬ　ぼたんかな
hō hyakuri / amagumo yosenu / botan kana
for a hundred leagues around — / no rain cloud approaches: / the peony
New Year
君が代や　二三度し　たると　とし忘れ
kimi ga yo ya / ni san do shi / taru to / toshi wasure
the age of our lord — / two or three times already / we have forgotten the year
