Nakatsuka Ippekirō (1887–1946) was a pioneer of free-verse haiku alongside Ogiwara Seisensui. Born in Osaka, he studied under Kawahigashi Hekigodō before moving toward a more radical position — rejecting seasonal references and fixed forms more completely than Seisensui's circle. He founded the journal Kaisō and developed an abstract, modernist approach to haiku that was more austere and intellectually demanding than Santōka's confessional style. He represents an important experimental strand in twentieth century haiku that remains less widely read than the free-verse mainstream.
