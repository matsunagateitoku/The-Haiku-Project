Kitamura Kigin (1624–1705) was a scholar, haiku poet, and teacher who bridged the classical literary tradition and the emerging haiku world of the seventeenth century. Born in Ōmi Province (present-day Shiga), he became an authority on classical texts and produced respected commentaries on The Tale of Genji and The Pillow Book. He was one of Matsuo Bashō's early teachers, passing on a rigorous classical foundation that shaped Bashō's mature aesthetic. Kigin's own haiku follow the Teimon school's learned style rather than Bashō's later innovations, and he is remembered more as a scholar and teacher than as a poet.
