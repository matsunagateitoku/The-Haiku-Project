Natsume Soseki
夏目漱石  ·  21 poems
Spring
雀来て障子にうごく花の影
susume kite / shōji ni ugoku / hana no kage
When a sparrow arrives / they move on the sliding screen, / shadows of the blossoms.
梅ちるや月夜に廻る水車
ume chiru ya / tsukiyo ni mawaru / mizuguruma
Plum blossoms fall: / turning in the moonlit night, / a water wheel.
永き日や欠伸うつして別れ行く
nagaki hi ya / akubi utsushite / wakare-yuku
A long day: / I take over his yawning / when he leaves.
落ちざまに蝱を伏せたる椿哉
ochi-zama ni / abu wo fuse-taru / tsubaki kana
Falling to the ground / it has trapped a gadfly, / a camellia flower.
朧夜や顔に似合ぬ恋もあらん
oboroyo ni / kao ni niawanu / koi mo aran
Hazy moonlight night: / there must be love for / unlikely faces too.
菫程小さき人に生れたし
sumire hodo / chiisaki hito ni / umare-tashi
Into a man / as tiny as a violet / may I be reborn!
有耶無耶の柳近頃緑也
uyamuya no / yanagi chikagoro / midori nari
That inconspicuous / willow tree—of late it has / become green!
Summer
寂として掾に鋏と牡丹哉
jaku toshite / en ni hasami to / botan kana
It is quiet— / on the veranda, clippers / and peonies.
別るるや夢一筋の天の川
wakaruru ya / yume hitosuji no / ama-no-gawa
I take my leave: / in my dream there stretches a streak— / the River of Heaven.
芝草や陽炎ふひまを犬の夢
shibakusa ya / kagerou hima wo / inu no yume
The grassy lawn: / amid the shimmering heat waves / a dog’s dream.
Autumn
秋の空名もなき山の愈高し
aki no sora / na mo naki yama no / nao takashi
Against the autumn sky / a nameless mountain towers / higher than ever.
顔洗ふ盥に立つや秋の影
kao arau / tarai ni tatsu ya / aki no kage
In the basin, / as I wash my face, there rises / autumn’s shadow.
肩に来て人なつかしや赤蜻蛉
kata ni kite / hito natsukakashi ya / aka-tonbo
It comes to my shoulder / longing for human company: / a red dragonfly.
Winter
炭竃に葛這ひ上る枯れながら
sumigama ni / kazura hai-agaru / kare nagara
Onto a charcoal kiln / a vine keeps climbing, while / being burnt to death.
時雨るるや泥猫眠る経の上
shigururu ya / doroneko nemuru / kyō no ue
Is it showering? / A muddy cat is asleep / on a Buddhist sutra.
烏飛んで夕日に動く冬木かな
karasu tonde / yūhi ni ugoku / fuyuki kana
The crow has flown away. / swaying in the evening sun, / a leafless tree.
眠らざる僧の嚔や夜半の梅
nemurazaru / sō no kusame ya / yowa no ume
Not yet asleep, / a Buddhist monk sneezes: / midnight plum blossoms.
寒徹骨梅を娶ると夢みけり
kantekkotsu / ume wo metoru to / yumemi-keri
The piercing cold— / I marry a plum blossom / in a dream.
New Year
元旦に生まれぬ先の親恋し
ganjitsu ni / umarenu saki no / oya koishi
On New Year’s Day / I long to meet my parents / as they were before my birth.
Unclassified
kiri ki naru / ichi ni ugoku ya / kagebōshi
See how it hovers / In these streets of yellow fog, / A human shadow.
winter
凩や海夕日を吹き落す
kogarashi ya / umi ni yūhi wo / fuki-otosu
The wintry gust: / it blows the evening sun down / into the ocean.
