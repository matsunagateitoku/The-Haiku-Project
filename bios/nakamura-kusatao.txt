Nakamura Kusatao (1901–1983) was a major haiku poet of the Shōwa era. Born in Chiba Prefecture, he became associated with the humanist tendency in mid-century haiku. He co-founded the magazine Banryoku and developed a style that combined classical rigor with attentiveness to human experience — relationships, emotion, and the presence of people within the natural world. He is one of the key transitional figures between the early Hototogisu school and the more humanly engaged haiku of the post-war era.
