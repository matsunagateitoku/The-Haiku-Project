Kubota Mantarō (1889–1963) was a poet, playwright, novelist, and haiku poet particularly associated with the shitamachi (old downtown) culture of Tokyo. Born in Asakusa, he spent much of his career writing about the city of his childhood — its atmosphere, seasonal rhythms, and passing way of life. His haiku are noted for their lyrical evocation of Tokyo's seasons and streets and for a tone of gentle nostalgia. He is also an important figure in twentieth century Japanese drama and light fiction.
