しし舞もせよやぼたんの花見酒
shishi mai mo / se you ya botan no / hanami-zake
dance that lion dance— among peony blossoms sipping blossom-viewing sake
Saito Tokugen
寂として掾に鋏と牡丹哉
jaku toshite / en ni hasami to / botan kana
It is quiet— on the veranda, clippers and peonies.
Natsume Soseki
牡丹一弁一弁の動きつつ開きつつ姿ととのう
botan / ichiben ichiben no / ugoki-tsutsu hiraki-tsutsu / sugata totonou
Peony: one petal, another petal, moving, opening, puts itself in order.
Ogiwara Seisensui
牡丹しろし人倫を説く眼はなてば
botan shiroshi / jinrin wo toku / me hanateba
How white the peonies as I stop sermonizing and free my eyes!
Iida Dakotsu
蟻地獄陰して牡丹花赤き
arijigoku / kage shite botan / hana akaki
An ant lion’s pit is hidden underneath a peony blossoming in red.
