Hasegawa Kanajo (1887–1969) was a major female haiku poet and a disciple of Takahama Kyoshi. Born in Yamagata Prefecture, she became an important figure in the Hototogisu circle and later founded the haiku journal Haiku Kenkyū. Her poems are known for their sensitivity to landscape and seasonal change and for a quality of reserved emotional depth. She was active as both a poet and a critic, and played a significant role in promoting haiku as a serious literary form for women.
