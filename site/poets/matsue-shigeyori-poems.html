Matsue Shigeyori
松江 重頼  ·  13 poems
Spring
やあしばらく花に対して鐘撞く事
ya shibara ku / hana ni taishite / kane tsuku koto
wait—hold on! / facing the blossoms / strike the bell
さほひめやかくる柳の長かもじ
saho hime ya / kakuru yanagi no / naga kamoji
The goddess Sao— / hidden in the willow, / her long hair.
鶯が引琴の緒や糸柳
uguisu ga / hiki koto no o ya / itoyanagi
The warbler / plays the harp strings / of a thread willow.
観音のちからや見するこぶ柳
Kannon no / chikara ya misuru / kobu yanagi
Kannon’s / strength seen in / a swelling willow.
春の日の威光をみする雪間哉
haru no hi no / ikō wo misuru / yukima kana
the spring sun / shows its authority / in in gaps in snow
料理して今日もくらしつ桜だひ
ryōri shite / kyō mo kurashitsu / sakura-dai
cooking / today, again, passing the day / cherry sea bream
落汐に鳴門やつれて暮の春
ochi shio ni / Naruto yasurete / kure no haru
at the ebbing tide / Naruto grows weary— / spring's end
Summer
順礼の棒ばかり行く夏野かな
junrei no / bo bakari yuku / natsuno kana
nothing but their staffs / going on ahead— / pilgrims through the summer fields
Autumn
秋や今朝一足に知るのごひ縁
aki ya kesa / hito ashi ni shiru / nogoi en
autumn this morning— / known in a single step / on the wiped veranda
しばし見む蟻の通路蝉のから
shibashi mimu / ari no kayoiji / semi no kara
let me watch awhile: / an ant's thoroughfare / and a cicada's shell
野に嬉し虫待宵の小行灯
no ni ureshi / mushi machi yoi no / koandon
how glad in the fields— / waiting for insects / the small lantern at dusk
御座舟や霧間もれたる須磨明石
gozabune ya / kiri ma moretaru / Suma Akashi
the royal barge— / through gaps in the fog / Suma, Akashi
Winter
ふる雪は京おしろいとみやこ哉
furu yuki wa / kyo oshiroi to / miyako kana
falling snow / is the capital's face powder— / such a city
