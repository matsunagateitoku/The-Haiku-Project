Nozawa Bonchō (?–1714) was one of Bashō's closest disciples, a physician from Kanazawa who settled in Kyoto. He is best known for co-editing the anthology Sarumino (The Monkey's Raincoat, 1691) together with Mukai Kyorai — a collection widely regarded as the finest product of Bashō's circle and an essential document of mature Edo-period haiku. His own poems are noted for their directness and sensory precision. His birth year is unknown, and after Bashō's death his profile in the haiku world diminished; his reputation rests almost entirely on his contributions to Sarumino.
