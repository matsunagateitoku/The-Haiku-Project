Ogiwara Seisensui (1884–1976) was the leading theorist of the free-verse haiku movement and founder of the influential journal Sōun (1911). Born in Tokyo, he studied under Kawahigashi Hekigodō and became convinced that haiku could achieve greater expressive range by abandoning both the 5-7-5 syllable structure and the obligatory seasonal reference. Through Sōun he published and mentored free-verse haiku poets including Taneda Santōka and Ozaki Hōsai, who became the movement's most celebrated practitioners. Seisensui continued editing and writing well into his eighties, outliving most of his contemporaries by decades.
