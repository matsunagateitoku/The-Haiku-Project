Nishiyama Sōin (1605–1682) was the founder of the Danrin school of haiku and the primary rival to Matsunaga Teitoku's dominant Teimon school. Born in Higo Province (present-day Kumamoto), he eventually settled in Osaka, where he built a large following. Against the Teimon emphasis on classical learning and verbal wit, the Danrin school promoted speed, vernacular energy, and comic invention. Sōin himself was a prolific composer who could produce verse rapidly and with evident ease. His school's influence waned after Bashō's rise, but it shaped the literary environment in which Bashō developed, and figures like Ihara Saikaku emerged from it.
