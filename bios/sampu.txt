Sugiyama Sanpū (1647–1732), known by his haiku name Sampū, was one of Bashō's most important disciples and his primary patron during the Edo years. A prosperous fish merchant in Nihonbashi, Edo, he provided Bashō with a small cottage in the Fukagawa district — the original Bashō-an, from which Bashō eventually took his pen name after planting a banana tree (bashō) on the grounds. Sanpū's support allowed Bashō to live as a poet and teacher rather than a working professional. His own haiku are competent but not distinguished; his significance in the tradition is primarily as the man who made Bashō's creative life in Edo financially possible.
