Natsume Sōseki (1867–1916) is best known as one of the most important novelists in Japanese literary history — the author of Kokoro, I Am a Cat (Wagahai wa Neko de Aru), and Botchan — but he was also a haiku poet in his younger years. He became a close friend of Masaoka Shiki at Tokyo Imperial University and composed haiku under Shiki's influence in the 1890s. He incorporated haiku into his novel Kusamakura (The Three-Cornered World, 1906), which centers on the ideal of a poet's detached observation of the world. As his fiction career developed he wrote less verse, but his engagement with haiku left a permanent mark on his prose style.
