Iida Dakotsu
飯田 蛇笏  ·  26 poems
Spring
なにもゐぬ雪水ふかくうごきけり
nani mo inu / yuki mizu fukaku / ugoki-keri
No creature is there— / And yet, deep in the thawing stream, / I have seen a stir.
Summer
幽冥へおつるおとあり灯取虫
yūmei e / otsuru oto ari / hitorimushi
To the Sea of Death / it plunged with a splash— / a tiger moth.
蚊の声や夜深くのぞく掛け鏡
ka no koe ya / yo fukaku nozoku / kakekagami
A mosquito’s whine: / in the depth of night I peer / into a hanging mirror.
遊泳やおぼるる水のかんばしき
yūei ya / oboruru mizu no / kanbashiki
Out for a swim: / the water that drowns people / smells fragrant to me.
夏真昼死は半眼に人をみる
natsu mahiru / shi wa hangan ni / hito wo miru
High noon in summer: / Death, with half-closed eyes, / observes a man.
みめよくて　にくらしき子や　天瓜粉
Mime yokute / nikurashiki ko ya / tenkafun
Nice looking, / but a detestable child! / Talcum powder.
Autumn
負馬の眼のまじと人を視る
make uma no / me no majimaji to / hito wo miru
The eyes of a horse / that lost the race—how intently / they stare at men.
Winter
月入れば北斗をめぐる千鳥かな
tsuki ireba / hokuto wo meguru / chidori kana
As the moon has sunk / they fly round the Big Dipper— / the plovers.
極寒のちりもとどめず巌ぶすま
gokkan no / chiri mo todomezu / iwa-busuma
In the extreme cold / not a particle of dust / on the towering rock.
雪山をはひまはりゐるこだまかな
setsuzan wo / hai-mawari iru / kodama kana
Snow-covered mountains— / crawling about for a long while, / echoes and re-echoes.
冬の蟇川にはなてばおよぎけり
fuyu no hiki / kawa ni hanatebe / oyogi-keri
In winter, a toad. / I set it free in the river— / it begins to swim!
いのちつきて薬香さむくはなれかり
inochi tsukite / yakukō samuku / hanare-keri
His life has ended— / the medicine’s smell coldly / leaves the body.
凪ぎわたる地はうす眼して冬に入る
nagi-wataru / chi wa usume shite / fuyu ni iru
The perfectly still / earth, with eyes almost closed, / enters on winter.
New Year
父祖の地に闇のしづまる大晦日
fuso no chi ni / yami no shizumaru / ōmisoka
In my forefathers’ land / darkness rests in calm / this New Year’s Eve.
Unclassified
gokukan no / chiri mo todomezu / iwabusuma
Walls of stone / Do not hold back even the dust / Of the dead of winter.
oba yuite / karuki itami ya / waka kaede
A light stab of pain / When my aunt passed away… / The young maples.
imo no tsuyu / renzan kage wo / tadashū su
Dew on the taro leaf: / The chains of the hills and mountains / Correctly range their forms.
summer
夏めくや霽れ雷のひとつぎり
natsu meku ya / hare kaminari no / hitotsu-giri
summary- / in clear skies, thunder / one bolt
瀧おもて雲おしうつる立夏かな
taki omote / kumo oshi utsuri / rikka kana
on the waterfall's surface / clouds pass over / start of summer
眼近くの葵に蛇や昼寝覚
majikaku no / aoi ni hebi ya / hirune-zame
Before my eyes / a hollyhock; on it a snake. / I wake from a nap.
牡丹しろし人倫を説く眼はなてば
botan shiroshi / jinrin wo toku / me hanateba
How white the peonies / as I stop sermonizing / and free my eyes!
紫陽花に八月の山たかからず
ajisai ni / hachigatsu no yama t / akakarazu
For the hydrangea / August mountains are never / sufficiently high.
落日に蹴合へる鶏や鳳仙花
rakujitsu ni / keaeru tori ya / hōsenka
In the setting sun / cocks are engaged in a duel: / Touch-me-nots in bloom.
autumn
白菊のしづくつめたし花鋏
shiragiku no / shizuku tsumetashi / hana-basami
White chrysanthemums— / how cold the dewdrops are / on my garden shears!
たましひのしづかにうつる菊見かな
tamashii no / shizuka ni utsuru / kikumi kana
My soul moves along / quietly among the flowers— / a chrysanthemum exhibit.
winter
死病えて爪うつくしき火桶かな
shibyō ete / tsume utsukushiki / hioke kana
Sick unto death, / How lovely her fingernails / on the wooden brazier!
