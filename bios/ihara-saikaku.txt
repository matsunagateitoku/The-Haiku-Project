Ihara Saikaku (1642–1693) is best known as the founder of the ukiyo-zōshi genre of popular prose fiction, but he was also a remarkable haiku poet. Born in Osaka, he joined the Danrin school and became famous for his capacity to compose verse at extraordinary speed: in 1684 he reportedly dictated over twenty thousand haiku in a single day, a feat more of endurance than artistry. His prose novels — including Kōshoku ichidai otoko (The Life of an Amorous Man, 1682) — brought the vernacular energy and worldly wit of Danrin haiku into extended narrative form, and it is as a novelist that he left the deepest mark on Japanese literature.
