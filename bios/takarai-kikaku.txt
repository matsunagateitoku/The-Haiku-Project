Takarai Kikaku (1661–1707), whose family name was Enomoto, was the most talented and most unconventional of Bashō's disciples. Born the son of a physician in Edo, he joined Bashō's circle as a young man and quickly distinguished himself by the brilliance and wit of his verse. Where Bashō cultivated sabi and spiritual restraint, Kikaku's haiku tend toward the sensuous and theatrical — closer in spirit to the Danrin school than to his master's mature aesthetic. The two remained close despite their differences, and Kikaku was among the disciples present at Bashō's deathbed in Osaka in 1694. After Bashō's death, Kikaku led one of the most active haiku groups in Edo.
